### Course Information
*   **Unit:** CSA-301-U1

*   **Duration:** April 27 – May 1
*   **Difficulty:** Beginner

---

### 1. Lab Objective
The primary objective of this lab is to build a strong theoretical and analytical foundation in Security Operations Center (SOC) concepts. Students will explore how SOCs are structured, the roles within them, and the frameworks used to analyze and categorize adversary behavior.

### 2. Topics Covered
*   **SOC Organizational Structures:** Centralized, Distributed, Virtual, and Co-managed models.
*   **SOC Roles:** Responsibilities of Tier 1 (Triage), Tier 2 (Incident Response), and Tier 3 (Threat Hunting/SME).
*   **SOC Workflows:** The lifecycle of an alert from ingestion to remediation.
*   **Threat Landscape:** Understanding common threat actors (Nation-states, Cybercriminals, Hacktivists).
*   **Analytical Frameworks:** Detailed study of the Cyber Kill Chain and MITRE ATT&CK.
*   **Operational Procedures:** Alert triage, severity levels, and escalation paths.

### 3. Required Resources

### 4. Lab Activities

#### Activity 4.1: Research Exercise - Analyzing a Recent Cyber Attack
Students must select a major cyber attack from the last 24 months (e.g., SolarWinds, Kaseya, or a specific high-profile ransomware incident).

**Instructions:**
1.  **Executive Summary:** Provide a one-paragraph summary of the attack (Who, What, When, and Impact).
2.  **Framework Mapping:** Analyze the attack through the lens of the **MITRE ATT&CK Framework**.
